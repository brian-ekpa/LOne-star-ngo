Who We Are

A Beacon of Hope for Nigeria's Most Vulnerable

Lone Star Hope Initiative (LSHI) is a non-profit organization established to promote the protection, rehabilitation, education, and psychosocial wellbeing of vulnerable street children and adolescents in Nigeria.

With particular emphasis on the girl child and youth affected by substance use, we work tirelessly to restore dignity, opportunity, and hope to those who need it most.

Our holistic approach addresses the root causes of street life — including family breakdown, poverty, and substance dependency — through evidence-based programs and compassionate care.

The Girl Child First

LSHI places the protection and empowerment of the girl child at the heart of everything we do. Street-connected girls face disproportionate risks of exploitation, abuse, and exclusion from education.

Our gender-responsive programs are designed to address these specific vulnerabilities — providing safe spaces, trauma-informed care, and pathways to sustainable futures for girls who have been failed by systems meant to protect them.

Our commitments to the girl child:

  • Safe and gender-sensitive shelter and care
  • Psychological counselling and trauma support
  • Scholarship and education access programs
  • Vocational skills for economic independence

💙

Substance Recovery

Substance dependency is one of the most critical challenges facing street-connected youth in Nigeria today. LSHI operates structured, evidence-based recovery programs that treat addiction not as a moral failing, but as a health issue deserving compassionate intervention.

We partner with certified counsellors, medical professionals, and community leaders to deliver care that addresses both the physical and psychological dimensions of substance dependency.

Our Drug rehabilitation pillars:

  • Professional clinical assessment and support
  • Peer mentorship and group therapy sessions
  • School-based prevention and awareness programs
  • Post-recovery livelihood and reintegration support
